NSO Group — Pegasus Zero-click spyware. Used on Jamal Khashoggi's associates before his murder. Found on 14 heads of state including Macron and Sanchez. Court ordered NSO to pay $168M to WhatsApp in May 2025.

Founded by ex-Unit 8200 officers. Pegasus infects a phone with no click required. Once installed: reads all messages including Signal and WhatsApp, activates microphone and camera, tracks location, extracts passwords. Every export must be approved by Israel's Defence Ministry — making NSO a de facto arm of Israeli foreign policy.

The New York Times: "Israel's government has long seen Pegasus as a critical tool for its foreign policy" and "treated NSO as a de facto arm of the state." Israel licensed Pegasus to UAE and Bahrain as part of the Abraham Accords — surveillance technology as diplomatic currency.

Pegasus was used to spy on Saudi journalist Jamal Khashoggi's inner circle before he was murdered and dismembered in the Saudi consulate in Istanbul. It was found on phones of 14 heads of state including French President Macron and Spain's Prime Minister Sanchez. Spain's High Court closed its investigation into the hack in 2026 after Israel refused five cooperation requests.

In May 2025, a US court ordered NSO to pay $168 million to WhatsApp for exploiting their systems. In June 2026, Meta accused NSO of already violating the court's injunction — continuing to target WhatsApp users in Jordan and Lebanon.

Sources: Wikipedia: NSO Group · The Record: Spain closes NSO probe · BDS Movement spyware tracker

Rachel Maddow warned the spyware "can be covertly inserted into anyone's phone from a drone hovering over a protest."

Citizens across Austria, Belgium, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Germany, Greece, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, the Netherlands, Portugal, Spain, and Sweden have been identified as Paragon targets, per a February 2025 European Parliament inquiry.
